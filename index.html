About me

I'm a Product Designer at Palantir Technologies and a CS grad from King's College London currently based in the Bay Area. Here are a few thing's I've been upto recently:

Palantir Technologies

On a day to day, I'm creating user-driven experiences for some of the most important government, commercial, and non-profit institutions. I've worked on a wide variety of projects ranging from philanthropic work, internal tools, to powerful data analysis apps.

HackCampus

A non-profit I co-founded in College with Index Ventures. We built a 10-week paid internship programme for students in the UK where they were matched with startups, given free accomodation in London for the duration of the internship, and a YC-style batch experience.

KCL Tech

An award-winning student society I started in College, which ran one of the UK's first inter-university hackathons. The society played a major part in connecting the UK tech scene.

I was also one out of the 100 recipients for the Web Summit Future Global Leader Scholarship, received the Jelf Medal — one of King's College London's most prestigious undergraduate awards, helped Codecademy launch their Year of Code campaign in the UK, led a QA team at SuperAwesome releasing a product to millions of users worldwide, and once upon a time blogged about Apple news and apps for AppAdvice.
